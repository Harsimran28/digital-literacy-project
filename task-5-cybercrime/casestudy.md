## What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers disguise themselves as trusted
individuals or organisations — such as banks, government agencies, or popular
websites — to trick victims into revealing sensitive information like passwords,
OTPs, credit card numbers, or Aadhaar details. The term "phishing" comes from
the idea of "fishing" for victims using fake bait.

---

## Case Study: The Fake Bank Alert

### Background
Rohan, a 19-year-old first-year engineering student in Bhopal, received an
urgent SMS one evening that appeared to come from his bank. The message read:
*"Your account has been temporarily suspended due to unusual activity. Verify
your details immediately to avoid permanent closure: [link]"*

### How It Happened — Step by Step

**Step 1 – The Bait**
Rohan received the SMS while studying late at night. The message looked
completely legitimate — it had his bank's name, a professional tone, and
created a sense of panic by threatening account closure.

**Step 2 – The Fake Website**
He clicked the link, which opened a website that looked identical to his
bank's official website — same logo, same colours, same layout. The only
difference was the URL, which read "secure-banklogin.in" instead of the
bank's actual domain.

**Step 3 – Entering Details**
Feeling anxious, Rohan entered his account number, debit card number,
ATM PIN, and the OTP that arrived on his phone — believing the site was
asking him to "verify" his identity.

**Step 4 – The Attack**
Within minutes of submitting the form, Rohan received a real SMS from his
bank showing that ₹18,500 had been debited from his account in two
transactions. The attackers had used his credentials in real time to
transfer money out of his account.

**Step 5 – The Aftermath**
Rohan immediately called his bank to block his card but the transactions
had already been processed. He filed a complaint at cybercrime.gov.in
and called helpline 1930, but recovering the money proved difficult
as the transactions appeared voluntary on record.

## Who is Usually Targeted?

Phishing attacks commonly target:
- **Students and young adults** who are new to digital banking
- **Elderly users** who are less familiar with spotting fake websites
- **People who have recently made online transactions** (attackers buy data lists)
- **Job seekers** who receive fake offer letters asking for personal documents
- **College students** through fake scholarship or fee waiver emails

---

## Consequences

- **Financial loss** — money transferred out of accounts instantly
- **Identity theft** — stolen credentials used to open loans or fake accounts
- **Emotional distress** — victims often feel ashamed and anxious
- **Academic impact** — students who lose savings face pressure that affects studies
- **Legal complications** — difficult to recover funds as transactions seem voluntary
