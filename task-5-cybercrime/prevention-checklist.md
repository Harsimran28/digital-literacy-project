# Stay Safe Online — Cybercrime Prevention Checklist
### For College Students in India

## 🔐 General Online Safety

- [ ] **Always check the URL** before entering any personal details on a website —
      the real bank or government site will never have a random domain like
      "secure-sbi-login.in" or "verify-account.co"

- [ ] **Never click links in SMS or WhatsApp messages** claiming to be from your
      bank, NPCI, or government — always open the official app or website directly
      from your browser

- [ ] **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts —
      email, social media, college portal, and banking apps

- [ ] **Use strong, unique passwords** for every account — never reuse the same
      password across multiple platforms

- [ ] **Never share OTPs with anyone** — no bank, government agency, or company
      will ever call you and ask for your OTP under any circumstance whatsoever

- [ ] **Log out of accounts** on shared or public computers — especially in
      college computer labs or internet cafés

---

## 💳 Financial & UPI Safety (Specific to Indian Students)

- [ ] **Never scan a QR code to receive money** — QR codes are for making
      payments, not receiving them. If someone asks you to scan a code to
      "receive" a refund or prize, it is a scam without exception

- [ ] **Set a daily UPI transaction limit** in your banking app — this minimises
      damage in case your account is ever compromised

- [ ] **Never enter your UPI PIN on any website or form** — your UPI PIN should
      only ever be entered inside your official UPI app (GPay, PhonePe, Paytm)

- [ ] **Verify the recipient's name before confirming any UPI payment** —
      always check that the name shown matches who you intend to pay

---

## 📧 Email & Social Media Safety

- [ ] **Do not open attachments from unknown senders** — even PDF or Word files
      can contain malware that silently steals your data

- [ ] **Check the sender's email address carefully** — phishing emails often use
      addresses like "support@sbi-alert.net" instead of the real domain

- [ ] **Never post your phone number, Aadhaar, or college ID** publicly on any
      social media platform

---

## 🆘 If You Are a Victim — Report Immediately

| Channel | Details |
|---|---|
| **National Cyber Crime Portal** | cybercrime.gov.in |
| **Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** (available 24×7, free to call) |
| **Your Bank** | Call the number on the back of your debit card immediately to block transactions |
| **College IT Cell** | Report if your college email or portal credentials are compromised |

> ⚠️ **Act fast** — the sooner you report financial fraud, the higher the chance
> of freezing the transaction before money is moved further.
