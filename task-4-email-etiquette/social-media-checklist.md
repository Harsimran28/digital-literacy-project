# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

**Created by:** Harsimran Singh Gurudatta | 25BAI10007 | B.Tech CSE (AI) | VIT Bhopal University

---

## ✅ DO'S

| # | Do This |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Keep your LinkedIn profile updated** — add every internship, certification, and skill you gain throughout college |
| 2 | **Use your real name professionally** — on LinkedIn and GitHub, your real identity builds trust with employers and collaborators |
| 3 | **Think before you post** — ask yourself "would I be comfortable if my professor or future employer saw this?" before posting anything |
| 4 | **Share your academic achievements and projects** — posting about things you built or learned shows initiative and passion |
| 5 | **Engage positively and constructively** — comment thoughtfully on posts, support peers, and contribute to meaningful conversations |
| 6 | **Use privacy settings wisely** — keep personal accounts private and professional accounts public |
| 7 | **Credit others when sharing their work** — always tag or mention the original creator when reposting content |

---

## ❌ DON'TS

| # | Never Do This |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Never post offensive, discriminatory, or hateful content** — even old posts can resurface and damage your reputation years later |
| 2 | **Never share personal information publicly** — avoid posting your phone number, home address, college ID, or Aadhaar details online |
| 3 | **Never badmouth your college, professors, or classmates** online — it reflects poorly on your professionalism regardless of the situation |
| 4 | **Never share exam papers, question leaks, or copyrighted study material** — this can result in academic disciplinary action |
| 5 | **Never engage in or ignore cyberbullying** — if you witness harassment online, report it rather than staying silent |
| 6 | **Never use social media during exams or important deadlines** — the distraction cost is far higher than it feels in the moment |
| 7 | **Never connect with strangers who ask for personal or financial information** — online scams often begin with seemingly friendly social media messages |

---

## 🔑 One Rule to Remember
> *Your digital footprint is permanent. Every post, comment, and like contributes to your online reputation — build it with the same care you would your resume.*
